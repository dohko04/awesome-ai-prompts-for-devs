# AI Bubble Developer Survival Checker

> **Trending:** Bloomberg, The Atlantic, and Norway's $1.7T sovereign wealth fund all warning of AI bubble risk (March 17-19, 2026). 25% chance of catastrophic deflation. Developers need to hedge.

## The Prompt

```
You are an AI Bubble Risk Advisor for software developers. Analyze my current tech stack, skills, and career position to assess my exposure to a potential AI bubble burst.

## My Context
- Current role: [YOUR_ROLE]
- Primary tech stack: [YOUR_STACK]
- AI tools I depend on: [LIST_TOOLS — e.g., Cursor, Copilot, Claude Code]
- % of my work that's AI-dependent: [ESTIMATE]
- Company's AI investment level: [LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH/ALL-IN]

## Analyze These Risk Dimensions

### 1. Skill Portability Score (1-10)
Rate how transferable my skills are if AI tool budgets get slashed overnight:
- Can I code without AI assistance at my current speed?
- Are my core skills (algorithms, architecture, debugging) still sharp?
- Do I have non-AI-dependent specializations?

### 2. Vendor Lock-in Risk
- How many of my workflows break if [specific AI tool] shuts down?
- Do I have local/open-source alternatives ready?
- Am I building on proprietary AI APIs with no fallback?

### 3. Career Hedge Score
- Am I known for "AI skills" only, or do I have durable engineering identity?
- Could I interview tomorrow without mentioning AI?
- Do I have projects that demonstrate fundamental engineering ability?

## Output Format
Provide:
1. **Risk Score** (1-10, where 10 = maximum exposure)
2. **Top 3 Vulnerabilities** — specific to my situation
3. **30-Day Hedge Plan** — concrete actions to reduce exposure
4. **"Bubble-Proof" Project Ideas** — 3 side projects that demonstrate durable skills
5. **Tool Contingency Map** — for each AI tool I use, list the free/open-source alternative
```

## Example Output

The prompt will generate a personalized risk assessment like:
- Risk Score: 7/10 (high AI dependency, single-vendor stack)
- Vulnerability: "90% of your debugging relies on Copilot — practice manual debugging weekly"
- Hedge: "Contribute to 2 open-source projects using vanilla tooling this month"
